What Are Mechanical Switches?

Mechanical switches are the individual mechanisms under each key on a mechanical keyboard. Unlike membrane keyboards, which use a rubber dome to register keystrokes, mechanical switches are built for precision, durability, and a satisfying typing experience.

Each switch type offers a unique combination of tactile feedback, actuation force, and sound. Understanding these differences is key to finding the switch that suits your needs.

The Three Main Types of Switches

1. Linear Switches

Feel: Smooth and consistent from top to bottom.
Sound: Quiet, with no tactile bump or audible click.
Best For: Gamers and those who prefer a fast, fluid typing experience.
Popular Options: Cherry MX Red, Gateron Yellow, Kailh Red.

Linear switches are ideal for gamers because they allow for rapid, repeated key presses without resistance. They‚Äôre also great for typists who prefer a quiet keyboard.

2. Tactile Switches

Feel: A noticeable bump when the key is actuated, providing feedback without being too loud.
Sound: Quiet to moderate, depending on the switch.
Best For: Typists and programmers who want a balance of speed and tactile feedback.
Popular Options: Cherry MX Brown, Zealios V2, Gateron Brown.

Tactile switches are a favorite among typists because the bump helps you know exactly when a key has been registered, improving accuracy and reducing typos.
