2. Understand Switch Types

The switch is the heart of your mechanical keyboard, and it determines how your keyboard feels and sounds. Here‚Äôs a quick refresher on the three main types:

Linear Switches: Smooth and quiet, ideal for gaming.

If possible, try out different switches before buying to see which one feels best for you.

3. Consider the Layout and Size

Mechanical keyboards come in various sizes and layouts. Here are the most common options:

Full-Sized (100%): Includes a numpad and all function keys. Best for those who need maximum functionality.
Tenkeyless (TKL): Removes the numpad for a more compact design. Great for gamers and those with limited desk space.
60%: Ultra-compact, removing the numpad, function row, and arrow keys. Ideal for minimalists and portability.
65%: Similar to 60% but includes arrow keys and a few extra keys for added functionality.

Choose a layout that fits your workspace and usage needs.
